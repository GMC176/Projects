Historical Places

The Pandavleni Caves, also known as the Trirashmi Caves or Nashik Caves, are a group of ancient Buddhist rock-cut caves located in Nashik.They were carved out of the basalt rock and served as a retreat for Buddhist monks.
The caves exhibit exquisite rock-cut architecture and intricate carvings. The interiors of the caves feature cells, chaityas (prayer halls), viharas (monastic dwellings), and water cisterns. The walls and pillars of the caves are adorned with beautiful sculptures of Buddhist deities, scenes from Jataka tales, and floral motifs.
The Pandavleni Caves showcase a blend of Hinayana and Mahayana Buddhist traditions.
Address: Pandavleni Road, Off Mumbai-Agra Highway, Nashik

Bhamer Fort is an ancient hilltop fort situated in the Satpura Range of the Dhule district in Maharashtra. The fort is located near the village of Bhamer, which is approximately 45 kilometers from the city of Dhule.Bhamer Fort holds historical significance and offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.
The fort is known for its strategic location and fortifications. It features remnants of ancient structures, including bastions, gateways, and walls. Exploring the fort requires a trek uphill, and visitors can witness the historical architecture and the scenic surroundings.
Bhamer Fort may not be as widely recognized or visited compared to some other forts in Maharashtra. It is advisable to seek local guidance or assistance while planning a visit to Bhamer Fort in Dhule district.
The fort stands at an elevation of about 1,107 meters above sea level and provides a vantage point to enjoy the natural beauty of the region.
Address: Satpura Range, Dhule, Maharashtra

Mehrun Lake, also known as Mehrun Talav, is a historic lake located near the Mehrun village in Jalgaon district of Maharashtra.The lake holds historical and archaeological importance. It is believed to have been built during the Satavahana period.
Mehrun Lake features a stone embankment and a circular structure in the center known as "Mor Mukut," resembling a peacock's crown. The lake is surrounded by lush greenery, providing a serene and peaceful ambiance for visitors. The lake area is adorned with stone carvings and inscriptions that date back to the Satavahana period. These carvings depict various motifs, including lotus flowers, animals, and geometric patterns.
Mehrun Lake is a popular spot for nature lovers and photographers. The picturesque surroundings and tranquil atmosphere make it an ideal place for relaxation and picnics.
Address: Jalgaon, Maharashtra
