Ramtek is a small town located in the Nagpur district of Maharashtra, India.Ramtek holds great religious and historical significance and is known for its ancient temples and scenic surroundings.
The town is famous for the Ramtek Temple, also known as Ram Temple or Shri Ram Mandir. It is situated atop a hill called Ramgiri, providing a picturesque view of the surrounding landscape. The temple is dedicated to Lord Rama, and it is believed to be the place where Lord Rama stayed during his exile.
Ramtek is home to a historic fort, known as Ramtek Fort or Ramgiri Fort. The fort has ancient ruins and offers a glimpse into its architectural grandeur from the past.
Ramtek is surrounded by scenic beauty and natural landscapes. The Ramtek town is situated near the foothills of the Satpura mountain range, offering opportunities for nature walks and treks. The nearby Khindsi Lake is a popular attraction for boating and picnicking.
Address: Ramtek, Nagpur District, Maharashtra

Ambadevi Temple is a prominent religious site located in Amravati, Maharashtra, India. It is dedicated to Goddess Amba, also known as Goddess Durga or Goddess Jagdamba.The temple showcases beautiful architecture with intricate carvings and sculptures. The main sanctum of the temple houses the idol of Goddess Amba, adorned with jewelry and traditional clothing.
Ambadevi Temple is considered a significant pilgrimage site, attracting devotees who come to seek the blessings of Goddess Amba.
Many visitors believe that offering prayers at the temple fulfills their wishes and brings prosperity and well-being.
Visiting Ambadevi Temple provides devotees and visitors with a spiritual experience and a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of Amravati. The serene surroundings, religious rituals, and the devotion of the devotees make it a significant place of worship in the region.
Address: Satpura Hills, Rajkamal Square, Amravati, Maharashtra

Lonar, located in the Buldhana district of Maharashtra, India, is renowned for the Lonar Crater and the surrounding Lonar Lake. While Lonar is primarily known for its geological significance, it also holds religious importance for certain communities. Lonar is home to the ancient Lonarka Devi Temple, dedicated to Goddess Kamalaja Devi.
Lonar finds mention in various ancient texts and scriptures, including the Skanda Purana and the Padma Purana. According to mythology, Lonar is believed to be the site where Lord Vishnu's Sudarshana Chakra (divine discus) struck the Earth, creating the Lonar Crater. Lonar is considered a significant pilgrimage site for devotees who worship Goddess Kamalaja Devi. Devotees visit the Lonarka Devi Temple to seek blessings, perform rituals, and participate in religious ceremonies.
The Lonar Lake is considered sacred by certain communities, and ritual bathing in the lake is believed to have purifying effects.
Address: Lonarka Devi Temple, Lonar Crater, Lonar, Buldhana, Maharashtra, India.
