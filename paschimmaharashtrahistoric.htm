Monuments And Forts

Pratapgad Fort holds immense importance in the Maratha history and is renowned for the Battle of Pratapgad fought between Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj and Afzal Khan in 1659.
Pratapgad Fort features a combination of Maratha and Mughal architectural styles. The upper fort houses various structures like the Bhavani Temple, a granary, chambers, and a statue of Shivaji Maharaj.
The Afzal Tower is a significant structure within the fort that marks the spot where Afzal Khan was killed by Shivaji Maharaj during their legendary meeting.
Address: Pratapgad Fort, Pratapgad, Mahabaleshwar Road, Satara District, Maharashtra

. Panhala Fort is perched atop the Sahyadri Mountain Range at an elevation of about 845 meters, providing stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys.: The fort showcases a blend of architectural styles influenced by different eras.
It features massive walls, bastions, gateways, granaries, water tanks, and various buildings that display the architectural prowess of the different ruling dynasties.: Some of the notable structures within the fort complex include the Teen Darwaza (Three Gates), Andhar Bavadi (Hidden Well), Sajja Kothi (Ambarkhana), Ambabai Temple, Someshwar Temple, and the Kalavanticha Mahal.
The Ambabai Temple, dedicated to the Hindu goddess Amba Bhavani, is an important religious site within the fort. It is believed to be one of the Shakti Peethas (divine abodes of goddess Durga) and attracts devotees from far and wide. Panhala Fort offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding landscape, including the lush green hills, forests, and the picturesque valley below. The sunset view from the fort is particularly mesmerizing.
Address: Panhala Fort, Panhala, Kolhapur District, Maharashtra

Shaniwar Wada is a historic palace fort located in the heart of Pune.Shaniwar Wada stands as a prominent landmark and a popular tourist attraction, offering insights into the rich history and architectural grandeur of the Maratha era.
It was built as a palace-fort complex to serve as the seat of the Peshwas. The fort witnessed several significant events in Maratha history until it was partially destroyed by a fire in 1828.Shaniwar Wada exhibits a mix of Maratha, Mughal, and Persian architectural styles. The fort complex originally had several buildings, including palaces, courtyards, gardens, audience halls, and residential quarters.
Ganesh Mahal is one of the prominent structures within Shaniwar Wada. It was the residential palace of the Peshwas and housed the administrative offices.Nagarkhana is the ceremonial entrance gate of Shaniwar Wada, where musicians used to play traditional tunes to announce the arrival of important guests.
Address: Shaniwar Peth, Shaniwar Wada, Pune, Maharashtra

Shiveneri Fort holds great significance as the birthplace of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, the founder of the Maratha Empire. Shivneri Fort is not only renowned for its historical importance but also for its impressive architecture and picturesque surroundings.
The fort features a sturdy fortification system with walls, bastions, and gates. It has seven entrance gates, including the main entrance called the Kadelot Gate. Inside the fort, there are various structures, including residential buildings, water tanks, temples, and a statue of Jijabai, Shivaji Maharaj's mother. Shivneri Fort is home to the Shivai Devi Temple, dedicated to Goddess Shivai. It holds immense religious significance and is considered the family deity of the Shivaji Maharaj's dynasty. Shivneri Fort is a popular destination for trekking enthusiasts.The trek to the fort involves traversing through rugged terrains and scenic trails
Address: Shivneri, Junnar, Pune, Maharashtra
